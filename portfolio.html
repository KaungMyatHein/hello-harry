Design System Design Tokens Multi-Platform 2025

Building Multi-Level Design Systems for Scalable Product Growth

Maintaining one design system across three radically different surfaces — POS, web, and mobile — using a federated two-level architecture with a shared global foundation and platform-specific libraries.

70–90%
Faster Handover
100%
Design Consistency
View Full Case Study
What was broken

A single shared Figma library served POS terminals, web dashboards, and mobile apps simultaneously — three platforms with radically different interaction models, screen densities, and input methods. Brand changes triggered days of manual synchronisation across files, QA found inconsistencies every sprint, and new designers couldn't determine which component was canonical.

How I tackled it
  • Audited existing files — found 47 duplicate colour values and 31 near-identical components
  • Designed a two-level architecture: global foundation (reference tokens, brand identity) + platform-specific libraries (semantic tokens, adapted components)
  • Defined a three-tier token model: reference → semantic → component
  • Established governance model separating foundation team and platform team ownership
  • Documented POS-specific constraints: 48px touch targets, dark surface default, prominent error states
What shipped

A federated design system with one global foundation file and three platform-specific libraries (POS, web, mobile). Brand updates propagate automatically — one token change updates every screen across every platform instantly. Design-to-dev handover dropped from 3–5 days to under one day.

Jar Aye Child App — Making Hard Math Feel Tractable

Designing a HOTS mathematics learning app for 6–12 year olds aligned to the Singapore curriculum — keeping children in productive struggle without tipping into frustration or boredom.

What was broken

Most edtech for children fails at one of two extremes: gamify so aggressively that learning evaporates, or digitise classroom worksheets and wonder why retention is low. Jar Aye's challenge was harder — make rigorous multi-step mathematical reasoning feel tractable to a 7-year-old without diluting its cognitive demands.

How I tackled it
  • Led remote usability research with 5 children in Mandalay testing competitor products
  • Designed segmented PIN auth (not email) to remove failure modes for young users
  • Built 5-state question component: default, filled, correct, wrong, fixed — each deliberate
  • Surfaced the HOTS pedagogy as navigable interface structure, not a teacher guide PDF
  • Replaced streak mechanics with a calendar grid — forgiving for parent-controlled screen time
What shipped

A mobile learning app covering the full HOTS Singapore Math curriculum for Primary 1–6. Includes grade-filtered leaderboard, 4-tier mastery reporting, achievement calendar, Adventure Quest challenge system, and subscription state management (trial / expired / frozen).
